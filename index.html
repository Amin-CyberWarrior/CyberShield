How to Spot a Phishing Email

Phishing attacks are rising ‚Äî we break down the signs so you can stay safe online.

To spot a phishing email, look for urgent/threatening language, generic greetings (Dear Customer), poor grammar/spelling, mismatched sender domains (e.g., @gmai1.com), requests for personal info (passwords, SSN), unexpected attachments, and suspicious links (hover to check URL) that try to create panic or offer too-good-to-be-true deals. Legitimate companies won't ask for sensitive data via unsolicited email.

Penetration Testing Explained

What it is, why it matters, and how it improves your security posture.

Penetration testing (pen testing) is a proactive cybersecurity practice where authorized "ethical hackers" simulate real-world cyberattacks on an organization's systems, networks, or applications to find and exploit vulnerabilities before malicious attackers do, helping to strengthen defenses, ensure compliance, and prevent costly breaches. It's like hiring someone to try and break into your building to find weak spots in your security, offering insights to fix flaws in code, configurations, or processes.
