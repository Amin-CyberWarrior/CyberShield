Top 10 Ways to Protect Your Account from Hackers

  1. Use Strong, Unique Passwords:
    Create long passwords (12–16+ characters) using letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse passwords across multiple sites.
  2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):
    Turn on 2FA whenever possible. App-based authenticators are more secure than SMS verification.
  3. Beware of Phishing Attacks:
    Avoid clicking suspicious links or downloading unknown attachments. Always verify email senders and URLs.
  4. Keep Software and Devices Updated:
    Regular updates fix security vulnerabilities in operating systems, browsers, and apps.
  5. Use a Password Manager:
    Password managers help generate and securely store strong, unique passwords.
  6. Secure Your Email Account:
    Your email is the gateway to password resets. Protect it with a strong password and 2FA.
  7. Avoid Public Wi-Fi or Use a VPN:
    Public networks are risky. Use a trusted VPN when accessing sensitive accounts.
  8. Review Account Activity Regularly:
    Monitor login history and security alerts. Act immediately if you notice suspicious activity.
  9. Limit What You Share Online:
    Avoid oversharing personal details on social media that could be used to guess passwords or security answers.
  10. Log Out of Shared Devices:
    Always log out from accounts on public or shared computers and never save passwords on them.
